National Park Trivia

What was the first national park?
Yellowstone(Wyoming, Montana, Idaho)
Which national park has the deepest lake in the U.S.?
Cater Lake(oregon)-1,943 feet deep
What causes the blue haze for which Great Smoky Mountains National park is named?
Plants - The haze is the result of transpiration : the evaporation of water from plants leaves.
Which president signed the National Park Service into being in 1916?
Woodrow Wilson
Butch Cassidy and his Wild Bunch hid out in the canyons of which national park?
Canyonlands(Utah)-After robbing trains at the turn of the 20th century, Cassidy and his gang hid in the confusing web of canyons in southwest Utah.
An early 20th-century newspapers described which national park as offering 'all the adversity of hell without the inconveniences'
Death Valley(California and Nevada)-Death Valley is the driest and hottest place in North America, with less than two inches of rainfall each year and a record high temperature of 134f.
Carbon Glacier in this national park has the greatest thickness and volume of any glacier in the continental U.S.
Mountain Rainier(Washington)
Which national park includes the largest wilderness area east of the Mississippi River?
Everglades(florida)-The park is considered a swamp, but it was once a river 50miles wide and six inches deep. South Florida is the only place in the world where alligators and crocodiles live together.
The most massive single mountain on Earth is located in which national park?
Hawaii Volcanoes-The volume of Mouna Loa, in Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, is some 20,000 cubic miles
Which of the following national parks is the newest?
New River Gorge National park and Preserves, West Virginia

Fun Facts about National Parks

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

  • The park is home to two active volcanoes
  • Mauna Loa is the Earth's largest volcano and exceeds Mountain Everest in height.
  • Kilauea stands east of Mauna Loa and is considered the world's most active volcano.
  • Mauna Loa covers half of the island in the south central area abd it has been active for nearly 700,000 years.

Everglades
